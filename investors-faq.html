Q1/2026 results presentation

Summary of performance for the first quarter

Our operating profit was approximately 104 million baht, a 33% decrease compared to the previous quarter and a 77% decrease compared to the previous year. You may recall that last year we had an extraordinary profit in the first quarter, which caused the significant difference, but this year we did not have that kind of profit.

On a year-on-year basis, revenue decreased by 6% and operating profit decreased by 78% due to extraordinary gains in the first quarter of last year. The profit margin decreased from 9% to 2%, while EBITDA, a measure of cash flow generation, decreased by 35% from approximately 900 million baht to 588 million baht. Net profit was 103 million baht, a decrease of 78%. However, our net cash and short-term investments increased by 14% to 11.63 billion baht.

Key factors impacting first-quarter earnings

  • An additional 114.5 million baht was set aside because one of our customers in the United States, a hearing aid company, filed for Chapter 7 bankruptcy.
  • The PMS, SiC and silicon businesses in China weakened and were impacted by the New Year holiday, resulting in decreased revenue.
  • In Ayutthaya, we saw an improvement compared to Q4 last year and returned to operating profitability.
  • In Lamphun, EMS demand remained stable and operating profit was flat, excluding the aforementioned provisions.
  • In China, our IC business saw operating profit increase both quarter-on-quarter and year-on-year, driven by strong demand for EV modules.
  • Our EMS business in China declined, as last year's complex tariff situation led most American customers to relocate production; we moved those operations to other business units within the company.
  • In the United States, our RFID business improved but remained unprofitable despite increased revenue, due to a significant decline in profit margins.
  • In Cambodia, the relocation of manufacturing away from China brought increased revenue and maintained profitability.
  • Fluctuations in the Thai baht produced a loss of approximately 63 million baht in the quarter, relative to the average exchange rate in the second half of last year.
  • Cash reserves increased 14% year-on-year, but decreased 2% quarter-on-quarter.

Hana Micro Lamphun (EMS business)

Revenue remained stable at 2.2 billion baht but decreased 19% year-on-year, related to last year's switch to a consignment model — a decline that is now reversing and will begin to increase during the remainder of the year. The automotive sector weakened due to cost pressures and tariffs, while RF, test and measurement equipment, and computing remained strong. We set aside a special reserve of 114.5 million baht for a client of more than 17 years that entered receivership; however, revenue from this company accounts for less than 1% of the Hana Group, so the event did not significantly impact our business. Our medical business continued to grow strongly, and the pipeline of new projects for Hana Lamphun remains very promising.

Micro Packaging (EMS business in China)

This business was severely impacted by U.S. tariffs, causing many American customers to relocate production. Revenue decreased 8% quarter-on-quarter and 21% year-on-year. We implemented cost restructuring, including some personnel adjustments, to align costs with lower revenue. Despite the downturn, the EMS business in China is expected to remain profitable.

Factory in Ayutthaya (IC business)

In Q4 we experienced our lowest capacity utilization on record at approximately 43%, but in Q1 we saw improvement, with revenue increasing 5% both quarter-on-quarter and year-on-year and utilization improving from 43% to approximately 50%. We are seeing cyclical recovery and a return of demand, but face difficulty finding the necessary labour and technicians due to high competition in the Ayutthaya and Bang Pa-in areas. Our existing major customers will drive further recovery and improved utilization.

Factory in Jiaxing (IC business)

The IC business improved significantly, with sales up 16% quarter-on-quarter and over 100% year-on-year, largely driven by very strong EV module activity. Our standard IC business is improving, but as it is a low-margin industry, we selectively reduced some less profitable clients and cut costs to improve overall profitability. The business has returned to profitability following this restructuring, and we believe Jiaxing will remain strong and profitable in the coming quarters. Workload was high in mid-2025, decreased in Q4, and began to recover in Q1; we are confident it will rise from Q2 onwards.

United States (RFID business)

Although total revenue increased 30% quarter-on-quarter and 5% year-on-year, we were unable to turn a profit due to the sharp drop in inlay prices. This business is growing very rapidly and has attracted large companies, which has driven down prices. We anticipate needing to double the business this year to return to profitability — challenging, but achievable, as we have received many large new orders and are increasing capacity. We expect to return to profitability in the second half, possibly late Q3 or early Q4. The higher-margin tire-tag business has weakened but shows signs of recovery in the second half.

Cambodia

Our Cambodian factory has benefited from the relocation of business away from China and is progressing well. The ongoing Thailand–Cambodia conflict creates some inconvenience and increases logistics costs but has not halted the business; customers continue to support and grow with us. Sales increased 40% quarter-on-quarter and almost doubled year-on-year. New medical business will contribute to growth, and we are confident Cambodia will remain profitable throughout the year.

Overview of end applications

  • Telecommunications: declined, mainly due to the consignment model, and is expected to recover.
  • Industrial: beginning to recover after the turmoil caused by tariff issues.
  • Automotive: slight decrease.
  • RFID: revenue increased, but profit margins decreased.
  • Other: relatively stable.

PMS business in Korea

This quarter was another difficult one — up 21% quarter-on-quarter but down 36% year-on-year, with the Chinese market weakened particularly by the Chinese New Year holiday. We continue to invest heavily in marketing in India and Korea and are starting to see positive returns, with revenue growth anticipated in Q2. We will also focus on the AI-driven solid-state transformer business, which we believe has high potential.

The transition to solid-state transformers (SSTs)

A major challenge in building data centers is the enormous amount of electricity required. To support this demand, power companies are shifting from large, traditional magnetic transformers to solid-state transformers (SSTs), which are lighter and smaller, faster, bi-directional (ideal for renewable energy), more efficient, support smart-grid communication, and manage renewable energy and battery storage more efficiently.

The importance of silicon carbide

Silicon carbide (SiC) will play a crucial role in solid-state transformers because, compared to silicon, it offers much higher switching speed, greater efficiency, higher voltage tolerance and far better temperature resistance. As data centers consume so much power, this is one of the biggest challenges for the expansion of AI — without sufficient power it cannot function — and SiC is central to managing power consumption more efficiently.

PMS's technology roadmap

The roadmap shows our existing portfolio (blue) and new developments (red), notably MOSFETs, JFETs and SiC JFETs at very high voltages up to 1,200V, 2,300V and 3,300V, for use in solid-state transformers and applications such as railways and new power requirements. This will be developed by Power Master, expected in Q3 of next year, and should drive business towards the end of next year and into 2028.

Liquidity

We generated approximately 600 million baht in cash flow, down from 900 million baht. We spent 236 million baht, compared to 210 million baht last year, and this is expected to increase. As mentioned, cash increased 1.4 billion baht, or 14%.

Updated information about Phononic

Hana is building a fully automated, in-house-designed production line, currently undergoing testing and fine-tuning. The qualification process — internal certification, then the customer (Phononic), then their customers — is expected to complete by the end of June. The Phononic technical team visited Hana last month and were very impressed, and recently signed a second large-scale production agreement that doubles the previous order. The first phase of capacity increase takes place in the second half of this year; the second begins in Q1 next year, given equipment lead times. We are also discussing a next-generation Co-Packaged Optics (CPO) cooling project: as 1.6-terabyte GPUs run very hot and require cooling, Phononic has indicated that a successful TEC for this package would significantly exceed the volumes Hana has already agreed to produce.

A new AI-driven project

Hana has received a new AI-driven project, separate from Phononic, for an oscilloscope to test 1.6-terabyte optical transceivers. These devices monitor high-speed data transfer rates of 240 gigabits per second in data centers; they are expensive but in high demand, and our customer sells them to users such as Meta, Google, Amazon and Microsoft. The project begins in June, with staff training overseas, equipment arriving in Q3, capacity increasing in late Q3, and mass production hoped for early Q1 next year (or possibly year-end). Capex is relatively small, perhaps under $5 million, but working capital will be much higher. In revenue terms it is extremely significant — equivalent to a top-five customer by next year.

Summary of AI-driven activities

  • Cooling-equipment business for Phononic.
  • The newly acquired high-density PCBA business, commencing in the second half of this year and early next year.
  • Solid-state SiC transformer devices, expected in the second half of next year.

Summary and future trends

  • Lamphun: many exciting new AI-driven projects, with winning more projects a strong opportunity.
  • Jiaxing (EMS): business affected by tariffs has been resized to ensure profitability.
  • Ayutthaya (IC): growing with improving utilization, though employment challenges remain.
  • China (IC): profitability improved significantly, driven by EV modules and power-management activities.
  • Ohio, USA (RFID): challenged by declining margins, but expected to return to profit by year-end as new orders offset lower margins.
  • Cambodia: stable and profitable.
  • PMS: still expected to report losses this year, but market recovery and new projects should bring improvement in the second half and beyond.

Capital expenditure (CAPEX)

From a very low 626 million baht last year, we forecast a threefold increase this year, potentially exceeding 2 billion baht, depending on when new machinery arrives. If it arrives before year-end it counts as investment for this year; if not, for the beginning of next year.

Question & answer session

How would a prolonged war affect the company?
A prolonged war leads to higher energy costs and inflation, impacting consumer spending. If oil prices remain high until year-end, there are concerns it could affect demand for certain goods in 2027. However, AI-related businesses will not be affected, as their investments are in infrastructure.

What are the outlooks and trends for the RFID business?
The RFID business is growing rapidly, but large companies entering the market have lowered prices and impacted margins. The solution is to increase volume — the company needs at least 80–100% sales growth this year to return to profitability. We have received orders close to that target and are ordering additional machinery, so we expect to return to profitability by year-end.

Can the company expand its operations in Cambodia?
Yes — production capacity is available; the challenge is finding new business, not expanding floor space. As workload increases, we will move machinery from Lamphun to Cambodia. We will soon begin producing medical products (hearing aids) for our long-standing largest customer, who has shifted from remote controls to the medical business; this will be a growth driver.

Has the Q1 profit reached its bottom?
The company does not provide profit forecasts, so we cannot answer directly. We are not satisfied with Q1 but are confident we can improve as the outlook improves. Despite concerns about the war, we hope it will not affect us this year, and we remain confident the company can continue to grow.

When do you expect PMS to return to profitability?
PMS will continue to incur losses this year, but as business clearly improves we expect losses to decrease. The amount will still be significant, but we believe we will begin to see reduced losses in the second half — though the full year will remain a loss.

What percentage of revenue will come from Phononic?
We cannot disclose exact figures, but this business is very important and Phononic could become one of our top 5–7 customers within the next year — more if new projects are added.

What is the progress of the joint venture with PTT (FT1)?
As PTT is the major shareholder, we must comply with their announcements. The situation is unchanged; we continue to monitor the project, but it is currently temporarily suspended.

Can I request the presentation file?
We do not disclose the presentation file, but the session was recorded and is available on YouTube or the Stock Exchange of Thailand (SET) website, where you can review the slides.

What are the trends in the gross margin of the IC business?
Gross margin across all businesses is affected by two main factors: capacity utilization and exchange rates. IC utilization was very low in Q4 but began improving in Q1 and should continue through Q2 and Q3, despite recruitment challenges. The weakening Thai baht against the US dollar is beneficial, so positive movements in both factors will support improved gross margins.

Does the company anticipate a loss this year?
While Q1 results weren't great, we did not incur a loss, and the overall situation is improving. In principle, we do not anticipate a loss this year.

Are you concerned about other customers in financial difficulty?
For the customer requiring a bad-debt provision, we were unaware until they stopped paying, which was unexpected. Generally, at this time, we have not found any other customers in a worrying situation.

What are the risks of doing business with Phononic?
Risks always exist. One is that the product might fail qualification, which we consider low; the worst case would be a delay to the start of production, not a standstill. Another uncertainty is the actual size of demand, which Phononic reports as enormous. Considering the growth of companies like Nvidia, AI growth appears truly strong, and we are confident in their business — but no one knows the exact future.

Besides the three projects presented, are there other AI-related businesses?
We do have others, but as they are not yet large enough to significantly impact the company, we have not discussed them. We only highlight AI businesses clearly important to Hana's growth; these others have future potential, and if they grow we will be happy to discuss them.

What does Hana Microelectronics do?+

Hana Microelectronics is an electronics manufacturing services (EMS) and semiconductor assembly & test (OSAT) provider. The group manufactures printed circuit board assemblies, integrated circuit packaging, RFID and smart-tag products, and sensor and optical modules for customers across automotive, industrial, medical, telecommunications, and other markets.

Where does Hana operate?+

Hana is headquartered in Thailand and operates manufacturing facilities across Thailand, China, Cambodia, and the United States. Details of each site are on the Locations pages.
