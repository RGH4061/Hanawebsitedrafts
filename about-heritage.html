1970s
1978

A wooden house on Silom Road

Founded in Bangkok as Minerva Trading Co., Ltd. — thirty people assembling LED watch modules in a wooden house at Silom, Bangrak. Later renamed Minerva Electronics, then Hana Industries.

1979

Onto LCD watch modules

The workshop moved into LCD (liquid crystal display) watch module assembly, following the next wave of consumer electronics components.

1980s
1982

A factory for Swiss watchmaking

A new factory opened on Ngang-Linchi Road to assemble electronic modules for quartz analog watch movements, supplying a major Swiss watch group.

1983

Complete watch movements

Production expanded from modules to complete watch movements, distributed into the Hong Kong watch market.

1984

First step into semiconductors

Hana Semiconductor (BKK) Co., Ltd. was established — the first move into integrated circuit (IC) assembly and a deliberate step into higher-technology manufacturing.

1986

Expansion at Laksi

PCBA and quartz watch production moved from Ngang-Linchi to Laksi to make room for growing IC work. Hana Coil Co., Ltd. was established to manufacture coil winding.

1988

A new name, a clearer focus

"Hana Industries" became "Hana Microelectronics Co., Ltd." Coil winding moved from Laksi to Suthisarn. PCBA production started at the Laksi plant.

1989

A trading subsidiary

Hana Trading Co., Ltd. was established to import finished watches from Hong Kong.

1990s
1991

Consolidating in Laksi

Integrated circuit production moved from Ngang-Linchi to the Laksi plant, bringing semiconductor work onto a single site. The following year, the headquarters moved from Silom to Laksi as the business expanded into functional test — and Laksi has been Hana’s home ever since.

1993

Lamphun, and a public listing

PCBA, COB, and coil winding moved to a new facility in the Northern Region Industrial Estate in Lamphun, and Hana listed on the Stock Exchange of Thailand as Hana Microelectronics Public Co., Ltd. PCBA production began at the new site the following year.

1996

Hana Technologies, and a Shanghai subsidiary

Acquired 100% of Swire Technologies Ltd. (renamed Hana Technologies). Hana Microelectronics Co., Ltd. (Shanghai) was established — the first manufacturing presence in China.

1997

A fire, and a new IC plant in Ayutthaya

A fire at the Bangkok semiconductor plant caused approximately THB 300 million in damage. IC production relocated to the Hi-Tech Industrial Estate in Bang Pa-In, Ayutthaya — still home to Hana's OSAT operations today.

1998

Olin Interconnect Technologies acquisition

Acquired 100% of Olin Interconnect Technologies (a division of Olin Corporation, US), forming a new subsidiary: Advanced Interconnect Technologies (AIT).

1999

AIT merger, and a step into microdisplays

Hana Technologies merged with AIT, an IC manufacturer in Batam, Indonesia, for a 43% stake. Hana Microdisplay Technologies, Inc. was established in the US to manufacture LCoS components.

2000s
2003

A second China subsidiary, in Jiaxing

Hana Microelectronics Co., Ltd. (Jiaxing) was established in China’s Yangtze River Delta, around 100 km southwest of Shanghai — the group’s second China presence after Shanghai.

2005

Jiaxing, China — operations begin

Production began at the Jiaxing site in eastern China, with PCBA and integrated circuits as the lead products.

2007

Lamphun and Ayutthaya grow; AIT sold

Lamphun added a 10,000 m² building — a 60% floor space increase. Ayutthaya added 3,000 m² of capacity. AIT was sold to Unisem.

2010s
2011

Floods in Thailand

The Ayutthaya subsidiary was inundated by Thailand's flooding in early October — causing significant property damage and business interruption. The team rebuilt.

2013

A new subsidiary in Cambodia

Hana Microelectronics (Cambodia) Co., Ltd. was established at the Koh Kong Special Economic Zone — the beginning of a third manufacturing country for the group.

2014

Recovery, a new HQ, and Lamphun 2

The headquarters moved to a new site at Vibhavadi-Rangsit, Bangkok, on land owned by the company. Final flood insurance settlement was received. A second Lamphun branch, Lamphun 2, was established.

2015

Two new factories open

Lamphun 2 began operations in semiconductors and PCBA. In September, the Cambodia plant came online — initially assembling RFID access control readers and quartz crystals.

2017

Thailand capacity expansion

Lamphun 2 fitted out a second floor, doubling its manufacturing capacity. Ayutthaya added a three-story, 2,600 m² warehouse.

2017

Power Master Semiconductor, in Korea

Power Master Semiconductor Co., Ltd. (PMS) was established in Korea to manufacture IC power management devices — extending Hana’s manufacturing footprint into Korea.

2018

Lamphun 2 grows; Ohio renamed

Lamphun 2 added 6,000 m² of production. The Ohio subsidiary became Hana Technologies, Inc., reflecting a broader scope across LCoS and RFID device manufacturing.

2019

A cleaner subsidiary structure

Three holding subsidiaries — Hana Technologies Group, Hana Technologies Investments, and Hana Semiconductor Holdings — were dissolved. No gain or loss recognized.

2020s
2020

Ayutthaya keeps growing

Ayutthaya added 6,650 m² of office and production space to support continuing growth in demand.

2021

Into the RFID inlay OEM business

Hana Technologies, Inc. entered the RFID inlay OEM business — a deliberate move into higher-volume RFID manufacturing.

2023

Capital raise; a new home in Ohio

Completed a private placement of 80.48 million shares, raising THB 4.58 billion gross. Hana Technologies, Inc. relocated to Solon, Ohio, tripling manufacturing floor space.

2024

Investment in FT1 Corporation

Entered an agreement with NewVersal Co., Ltd. (PTT Public Co., Ltd.) to invest in FT1 Corporation, holding 49% after a further THB 744.8 million investment. Ayutthaya purchased adjacent land for THB 86 million.

2025

Cambodia land sale

In February 2025, the company sold land held for dormitory development in Cambodia for USD 1.3 million.
